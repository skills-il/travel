# Aviation Services Law: compensation and assistance reference

All figures are the in-force amounts as of 2026. The Aviation Services Law amounts are updated every January 1 by the change in the Consumer Price Index and rounded to the nearest 10 NIS, so always confirm the current-year figure before quoting it to a user.

This is Israel's own law (חוק שירותי תעופה, 2012, "חוק טיבי"). It is NOT EU Regulation 261/2004. Never use EU261 amounts (250 / 400 / 600 EUR) or the EU261 3-hour delay threshold for an Israel-nexus flight.

## Monetary compensation by flight distance (2026)

Distance is measured from the origin to the final destination (for a connecting itinerary, use the whole-journey distance, not a single leg).

| Flight distance | Compensation (2026) |
|---|---|
| Up to 2,000 km | 1,530 NIS |
| 2,000 to 4,500 km | 2,450 NIS |
| Over 4,500 km | 3,670 NIS |

Rough distance guide from Tel Aviv (confirm the exact great-circle distance for borderline cases):
- Up to 2,000 km: most of Europe's east and south (Athens, Larnaca, Istanbul, Bucharest, Rome, Vienna).
- 2,000 to 4,500 km: western and northern Europe (London, Paris, Berlin, Amsterdam, Madrid), and nearby.
- Over 4,500 km: North America, the Far East, southern Africa, Australia (New York, Bangkok, Johannesburg).

## When monetary compensation is owed

The passenger is entitled to the monetary amount above when any of these happens and no exemption applies:

- Cancellation: the airline cancelled with less than 14 days notice before the scheduled departure and did not offer a qualifying alternative.
- Delay treated as cancellation: the flight departed 8 or more hours late (international). For domestic Israeli flights the threshold is 3 hours.
- Involuntary denied boarding (overbooking): the passenger was bumped and did NOT volunteer to give up the seat.
- Early departure: moved earlier by more than 5 and up to 8 hours, with less than 14 days notice, gives a refund or alternative flight (assistance). Moved earlier by more than 8 hours also adds the monetary compensation above.
- Downgrade to a lower class than booked: compensation is a percentage of the ticket price the passenger paid, set by the law's Second Schedule (see the downgrade table below).

## Downgrade compensation (Second Schedule)

When the passenger is moved to a lower class than booked, the compensation is a percentage of the ticket price they paid (for a connecting itinerary it is pro-rated by the downgraded leg's distance):

| Downgrade | Percentage of ticket price |
|---|---|
| First class to Business | 60% |
| Business to Economy | 80% |
| First class to Economy | 90% |
| First or Business to Economy, on a flight over 4,500 km | 100% |

## The 50% reduction for an accepted alternative flight

If the passenger accepted an alternative flight the airline offered, the airline may pay only half the monetary amount, provided the alternative arrives within this window of the original arrival:

| Flight distance | Alternative arrives within |
|---|---|
| Up to 2,000 km | 2 hours |
| 2,000 to 4,500 km | 3 hours |
| Over 4,500 km | 4 hours |

There is also a no-compensation carve-out: if the airline gave 7 to 14 days notice and offered an alternative departing no more than 2 hours early and arriving no more than 4 hours after the original, no monetary compensation is owed (assistance and the refund/alternative choice still stand).

## Assistance obligations (owed even when money is exempt)

Assistance (שירותי סיוע) is a SEPARATE entitlement from the monetary compensation. It is owed during a delay even when an exemption removes the monetary compensation.

| Delay at the airport | The airline must provide |
|---|---|
| More than 2 hours | Food and beverages reasonable to the wait, plus communication services (up to 2 phone calls and one fax or email, the passenger's choice) |
| More than 5 hours | The above, plus hotel accommodation and ground transport when an overnight or long wait applies, OR the passenger's choice of a full refund or an alternative flight |
| 8 hours or more | Treated as a cancellation: the monetary compensation above is added on top of the assistance |

The passenger always has the right to CHOOSE between a full refund of the ticket and an alternative flight (rebooking) when the trigger applies.

## Exemptions (no monetary compensation)

- Extraordinary circumstances beyond the airline's control, provided the airline did everything it could to operate the flight anyway. Note: Israel's law and case law read this category more narrowly than airlines claim; a routine technical fault is usually NOT an exemption.
- Iron Swords (Amendment 2): airlines are statutorily exempt from the MONETARY compensation for flights in specific defined war-period date ranges. Assistance, refund, and the alternative-flight choice are still owed. If the flight date falls in a war-period window, check whether it is one of the exempted ranges before promising the money.

When an exemption applies to the money, the passenger still keeps the assistance and the refund/alternative-flight rights.

## Scope: when the law applies at all

- The flight departs from Israel, OR arrives to Israel (including itineraries with a stopover). This covers both Israeli and foreign carriers operating to and from Israel.
- Domestic Israeli flights are covered under separate regulations (3-hour delay threshold).
- If the flight neither departs from nor arrives to Israel and has no Israeli organizer, this law does NOT apply. For a flight within or departing the EU, EU261 may apply instead, with different amounts and a 3-hour threshold. Do not mix the two regimes.

## Claiming: the path

1. Send a written demand to the airline (the skill generates this letter). Attach the booking, boarding pass or check-in proof, and any delay/cancellation evidence (airline notice, airport board screenshot, SMS).
2. Cite the statutory payment deadlines so the demand has teeth:
   - The airline must refund the fare within 21 days of a written request (when the passenger chose a refund instead of an alternative).
   - The airline must pay the First Schedule monetary compensation (cancellation, long delay, early departure) within 45 days of a written request.
   - Downgrade compensation under the Second Schedule is paid within 21 days of the flight date.
3. If the airline refuses or ignores the demand, file a small claims suit (תביעה קטנה). Hand off to the israeli-small-claims-court skill.
4. In parallel, a complaint can be filed with the Civil Aviation Authority (the regulator) and the Israeli Consumer Council.

The fixed statutory compensation is separate from actual consequential losses. If the disruption forced extra spending (an unplanned hotel night, a missed onward connection, replacement tickets), those real out-of-pocket losses can be claimed in addition to the statutory amount, usually proven with receipts in the same small claims suit. Do not treat the fixed amount as the ceiling on what the passenger can recover.

The limitation period for a claim under the law is 4 years from when the entitlement arose.
